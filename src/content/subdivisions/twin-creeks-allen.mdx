Twin Creeks Allen roofing work requires more than a shingle crew. Homes in this neighborhood sit inside a North Texas hail corridor, carry visible rooflines, and often require exterior changes to respect neighborhood standards.

Pro Exteriors handles the inspection, documentation, material selection, and installation workflow for homeowners who need a roof replacement, storm-damage repair, or a second opinion after a major hail event.

## HOA-Aware Roofing For Twin Creeks

The right roofing recommendation starts with the existing home, the roof geometry, and any neighborhood requirements that apply to exterior materials or color. We document the roof condition, explain the system options, and prepare the information a homeowner needs before the project moves into approval or scheduling.

Architectural asphalt shingles remain the practical default for most homes in Twin Creeks. Class 4 impact-resistant shingles deserve a serious look because they can improve hail performance and may qualify for homeowner insurance discounts depending on the carrier and policy.

## Storm Damage Documentation

After a hail or wind event, the worst mistake is relying on a ground-level glance. Hail impacts can bruise the mat below the granules, damage ridge caps, dent vents and flashing, or create small leak paths that do not show up until the next heavy rain.

Our inspection process captures each roof plane, notes secondary damage, and gives the homeowner a written summary. If repair is enough, we say that. If replacement is the responsible call, the scope explains why.

## Start With An Inspection

Twin Creeks homeowners should start with the residential inspection route unless water is actively entering the home. Active leaks and open roof damage belong in the emergency intake path so the roof can be stabilized first.

Request a residential inspection and include the address, approximate roof age, and whether the request follows a specific storm date.
