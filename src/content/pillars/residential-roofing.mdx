faqs:
  - question: "How do I know if my roof needs a full replacement or just a repair?"
    answer: "Age, damage pattern, and shingle granule loss are the three deciding factors. A roof under 10 years old with isolated damage is usually a repair candidate. A roof over 20 years old with widespread granule loss, multiple leak points, or hail hits on more than 30% of the field is typically a replacement. An adjuster's report and an independent contractor inspection together give you the clearest picture — they often disagree, and knowing why matters."
  - question: "My HOA requires pre-approval before I replace my roof. How long does that process take?"
    answer: "Most HOA architectural review committees in DFW and Kansas City planned communities take 10–30 days to respond. Submit your material spec sheet, manufacturer color sample, and a photo of the existing roof simultaneously to avoid back-and-forth delays. We prepare the full submission package for homeowners in our service areas and track approval status so the project schedule stays intact."
  - question: "Will my homeowner's insurance cover a full roof replacement after a hail storm?"
    answer: "Coverage depends on your policy type (ACV vs. RCV), your deductible, and whether the damage meets your carrier's threshold for functional impairment. RCV policies pay replacement cost minus your deductible once work is complete. ACV policies depreciate the payout based on roof age. Document damage immediately after a storm — most carriers enforce a one-year filing window, and some enforce 180 days."
  - question: "What roofing materials hold up best in hail-prone markets like Denver and Wichita?"
    answer: "Class 4 impact-resistant shingles are the standard recommendation for high-hail-frequency markets. They carry a UL 2218 rating and often qualify for a 20–30% insurance premium discount in Colorado and Kansas. Metal roofing — standing seam in particular — also performs well against hail but carries a higher upfront cost. The right choice depends on your budget, remaining mortgage term, and resale timeline."
  - question: "How long does a residential roof replacement actually take?"
    answer: "A standard single-family replacement in our markets runs one to two days for the physical installation, assuming no structural decking repairs are needed. Permit issuance adds one to five business days depending on the municipality. Total project timeline from signed contract to final inspection is typically seven to fourteen days, weather permitting. We schedule around forecasted rain windows and communicate delays the same day they're identified."
  - question: "Do I need to be home during the roof replacement?"
    answer: "No. You need to be available for the pre-job walkthrough and the final inspection sign-off, but the installation crew works independently. We ask that vehicles be moved from the driveway and that pets be kept indoors or away from the property. Our project manager sends photo updates at tear-off, decking inspection, and completion so you have a documented record without needing to be on-site."
