  - question: "How do I know whether my commercial roof needs a full replacement or just repairs?"
    answer: "The decision hinges on three factors: remaining membrane life, deck condition, and total damaged area. If more than 25% of the roof surface is compromised or the deck shows moisture infiltration, a full replacement almost always costs less over a ten-year horizon than repeated repairs. A qualified inspector can core-test the insulation and probe the deck to give you a data-backed answer — not a sales pitch."
  - question: "Which roofing system holds up best in DFW and other hail-belt markets?"
    answer: "For low-slope commercial roofs in hail-prone markets, 60-mil TPO and EPDM membranes with a Class 4 impact rating outperform thinner alternatives in both claims frequency and long-term adhesion. Standing seam metal is the strongest option for steep-slope commercial applications. System selection also depends on your building's thermal movement, existing insulation R-value, and local code requirements — there is no universal answer."
  - question: "What does a commercial roofing inspection actually include?"
    answer: "A thorough commercial inspection covers membrane surface condition (blistering, splitting, granule loss on modified bitumen), seam and flashing integrity, drain and scupper flow capacity, penetration seals around HVAC curbs and conduit, and deck soundness via core cuts or infrared scanning. You should receive a written report with photos, not just a verbal summary."
  - question: "How long does a commercial re-roofing project typically take?"
    answer: "A standard 20,000–50,000 sq ft low-slope re-roof runs five to fifteen business days depending on system type, weather windows, and whether a tear-off is required. Projects requiring asbestos abatement or significant deck replacement add time. Your contractor should provide a phased schedule so building operations can continue during work."

We do not act as a public adjuster, but we coordinate closely with your team and carrier throughout the process."
  - question: "What maintenance program should a facility manager put in place after a new commercial roof installation?"

Most membrane warranties require documented maintenance to remain valid.
