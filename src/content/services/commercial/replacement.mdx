### What Commercial Roof Replacement Actually Involves

The replacement of a commercial roof is fundamentally different from adding another layer over the existing one or using patched solutions at scale. It begins with a thorough inspection. This involves a detailed assessment of the existing roofing assembly, which usually includes an infrared moisture survey, performing core cuts to evaluate deck structure and membrane composition and an examination of your roof drainage system to ensure it's adequate for current code. Skipping this initial assessment is a surefire way for contractors to win projects by submitting low bids, only to surprise owners with a change order later in the project.

After the assessment, we produce a written scope document that'll detail: the existing roof system, the recommended replacement system along with the rationale behind material selections, the approximate tear - off tonnage we'll be removing, any structural deck repair allowances we will include and drainage modifications necessary to meet current code. This document will then be the foundation of your contract, preventing any last - minute unpleasant surprises.

The roof tear - off is sequenced in single - day increments - only exposing the amount of deck that can be securely covered before the crew punches out for the day, thus protecting the building from weather exposure throughout the process. Next, a new single - ply membrane is installed meticulously following the manufacturer's detailed specifications. This includes proper adhesive rates, precise seam widths, termination techniques where membranes meet walls or penetrations and all penetration flashing details. Every critical detail is documented with a series of photographs for inclusion in your warranty file.

For flat and low - slope applications, TPO, EPDM or PVC membranes are typically specified from either Carlisle, Versico or Duro - Last. The final choice will depend on the building's thermal performance goals, its chemical exposure profile and budgetary constraints. If a project requires a high - performance vapor barrier or needs to accommodate a green roof system, we integrate Soprema's extensive line of modified bitumen membranes. For standing - seam metal retrofits or replacement projects, we install either Sentrigard or McElroy Metal panel systems. Every specification decision we make is documented and discussed thoroughly with the owner prior to ordering materials.

We then perform a full inspection of every seam. This is followed by an inspection of drain flow to ensure proper functionality, formal registration of your warranty with the manufacturer and the delivery of a close - out package to the owner. This package includes as - built drawings showing how the new roof was installed, the applicable manufacturer's and our installation warranty certificates and recommendations for future maintenance of the roof system.
