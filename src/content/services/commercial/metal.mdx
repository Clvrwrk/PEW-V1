## What Goes Into a Commercial Metal Roof?

Work begins with the first structural assessment to confirm the existing condition of the roof, load capacity testing of any substrates (commercial-grade metal systems have weight added), coordination with the structural engineer of record where appropriate.

Panels are chosen and sourced from McElroy Metal or Sentrigard (both are top commercial metal panel makers), which have full-range Galvalume and painted steel products, featuring a durable and reflective Kynar 500 PVDF paint coating-these meet Energy Star reflectance requirements and assist in obtaining LEED documentation if a customer desires it.

We even consult with and outsource work to specialized fabrication partners who will custom-build panels and other systems when a roof is so unique that a standard product isn't suitable (i. e. Curved roofs or standard systems that go directly over a structural deck of irregular shapes).

Following is our step-by-step approach to building a safe, resilient roof system: Substrate preparation (including vapor barriers or other required vapor control membranes by zone climate, insulation is layering over the vapor barrier), Clip installation per engineered spacing, Panel roll out and engagement, as it pertains to either snap-lock panel style or through hand crimping of the roof seams, Ridges and eaves are properly finished and secured, followed by any roof penetrations-like HVAC or roof hatch penetrations, every penetration includes two levels of flashing (the initial penetration detail and a second bead of sealant), This sequence closes with a comprehensive inspection of roof seams, thermal scanning and quality assurance testing before the roof project is declared officially completed.
