## What EPDM Roofing Actually Involves

EPDM, Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer, is a durable synthetic rubber compound used in manufacturing single - ply membrane sheets, typically ranging from 45 to 90 mil thick. Pro Exteriors specifies 60 mil as our standard for general commercial projects, while 80 mil is utilized for roofs requiring a more robust or high - traffic design. The key suppliers we source are Carlisle SynTec and Versico, which provide membranes with detailed tensile strength, elongation and UV resistance data that we use in developing project specifications.

Attachment method is one of the first significant design choices in EPDM projects. For fully adhered systems, we bond the membrane directly to the underlying insulation using bonding adhesive, yielding a strong, wind - resistant system that's ideal for high - wind environments such as Dallas - Fort Worth and Denver. Mechanically attached EPDM systems use fasteners and plates that are driven through the membrane and the insulation layer down to the structural deck - a faster and simpler attachment choice for many applications. Ballasted roofs are less frequently implemented in our primary regions, while viable over concrete structures, dead - load limitations generally render them impractical. River stones or pavers serve to secure the membrane.

Insulation choice is inextricably linked to membrane specification. Polyisocyanurate (polyiso) forms the standard insulation component of most EPDM assemblies. This is commonly paired with a coverboard made of high - density polyiso, gypsum or wood fiber. This coverboard enhances the protection of the membrane from puncture and improves membrane adhesion during install. Substrate conditions are examined prior to specification finalization, we pull cores out of the roof system to assess insulation thermal value, check for water or moisture ingress and review the deck structure to assess overall integrity.

Roof penetrations - curbs around pipes, walls, drains, HVAC equipment curbs - are the most common starting point for failures in EPDM roof systems, especially if field seams and components are improperly fabricated or installed. We typically specify Carlisle SynTec and Versico prefabricated flashing accessories whenever possible to reduce the number of seams constructed in the field, thus reducing leak potential. Each penetration utilizes two layers of flashing - first an uncured peel - and - stick base sheet, topped by a field - welded cap sheet of EPDM material - with all work documented photographically for quality control.

## Our Process

1. **Building Assessment and Core Sampling: ** After a thorough visual inspection of your current flat roof, we take cores to check insulation R - value, identify any trapped water or moisture in the roof layers and document the condition of the roof deck through photographs and detailed notes. We don't prepare any project specifications without fully understanding the current conditions of your roof system.

2. **Specification and Proposal Development: ** Based on the findings of our building assessment, estimators craft a written specification detailing the scope of the EPDM installation, referencing the relevant industry standards such as ASTM D4637, specifying requirements mandated by FM Global (when applicable) and detailing the particular Carlisle SynTec or Versico EPDM system components. Our detailed proposal offers line - itemized pricing for every aspect of the project, including membrane, insulation, flashings and labor costs - no mysterious bulk pricing that leaves you wondering what you're actually paying for.

3. **Manufacturer Pre - Job Notification: ** Before any on - site work begins, for projects where manufacturer warranty is desired, Pro Exteriors formally notifies Carlisle SynTec or Versico of the project. This step is mandatory in order for you to obtain your manufacturer warranty, unfortunately, it's a step many contractors overlook, voiding your manufacturer guarantee.

4. **Substrate Preparation: ** After your existing roof membranes are prepared for new installation, we replace any wet or damaged insulation and clear all debris. The deck is swept, visually examined and we address any high fastener heads or protrusions before installing any EPDM membrane.

5. **Membrane Installation and Quality Control: ** The seams are either heat - welded or solvent - bonded according to the specific EPDM system specification being installed and then checked with an probe tool. This work is then photographically documented. Flashings are installed and verified in place before any HVAC curbs or new equipment can be set.

6. **Final Inspection and Warranty Registration: ** A comprehensive, joint walkthrough is scheduled between the facility manager and Pro Exteriors management. Once the walkthrough is complete, the warranty registration is submitted to the manufacturer and a full project closeout package is handed over. This package includes "as - built" photos of the new roof installation, your manufacturer warranty certificate and suggestions for best maintenance practices.
