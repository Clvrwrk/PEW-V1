## What EPDM Roofing Actually Involves

EPDM membrane installation requires precise substrate preparation, proper insulation integration, and expert seaming techniques that determine long-term performance. We remove existing roofing materials down to the structural deck, inspect for moisture damage, and replace any compromised decking or insulation. The new EPDM membrane arrives in large sheets that minimize field seams — typically 10-foot or 20-foot widths depending on building configuration.

Membrane attachment methods vary based on building type and local wind loads. Fully adhered systems use contact cement or water-based adhesives for maximum wind resistance on high-profile buildings. Mechanically attached systems use specialized fasteners and cover strips for cost-effective installation on warehouses and manufacturing facilities. Ballasted systems rely on smooth river rock or concrete pavers to hold the membrane in place.

Seam welding represents the most critical installation phase. Our certified technicians use hot-air welding equipment to create molecular bonds between membrane sheets. Every seam undergoes destructive testing to verify bond strength before we move to the next section. Flashing details around penetrations, drains, and perimeter edges require custom fabrication using EPDM accessories and liquid-applied sealants.

Carlisle and Versico provide complete accessory systems including pre-molded corners, pipe boots, and walkway pads that integrate seamlessly with the membrane. We install tapered insulation systems that eliminate ponding water and improve drainage to roof drains. Metal edge details and gravel stops provide clean perimeter termination that resists wind uplift and thermal movement.

## Our EPDM Installation Process

1. **Pre-Installation Assessment** — Thermal imaging survey identifies moisture intrusion and structural issues that require attention before membrane installation. We coordinate with facility management to establish work zones and minimize operational disruption.

2. **Substrate Preparation** — Complete removal of existing roofing materials, deck inspection and repair, and installation of new insulation systems. Proper substrate preparation prevents 90% of premature membrane failures.

3. **Membrane Installation** — EPDM sheets positioned and attached using specified methods (adhered, mechanically attached, or ballasted). Our crews work in weather-appropriate conditions to ensure optimal adhesive curing and seam quality.

4. **Seaming and Flashing** — Hot-air welding of membrane seams with destructive testing verification. Custom flashing fabrication around penetrations, drains, and perimeter details using manufacturer-approved accessories.

5. **Quality Control Inspection** — Final walkthrough with thermal imaging verification of seam integrity and proper drainage flow. We document all installation details and provide comprehensive warranty registration.
