## What EPDM Roofing Actually Involves

EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer) is a synthetic rubber compound formed into single-ply membrane sheets at thicknesses ranging from 45 to 90 mils. Pro Exteriors specifies 60-mil as the standard for general commercial projects and 80-mil for roofs with significant rooftop foot traffic, hail-belt exposure, or elevated wind-uplift requirements. We source primarily from Carlisle SynTec and Versico — both publish detailed tensile strength, elongation, and UV resistance data that we use to write project-specific specifications.

### EPDM membrane thickness — when to spec which

| Thickness | Best for | Service life | Cost premium vs. 45-mil |
|---|---|---|---|
| 45-mil | Light-traffic roofs, low-budget retrofits | 18–22 years | baseline |
| **60-mil (Pro Exteriors standard)** | General commercial, moderate hail exposure | **25–30 years** | +15–20% |
| 80-mil | High-traffic, hail-belt, elevated wind zones | 28–35+ years | +30–40% |
| 90-mil | Specialty applications, fully ballasted | 30+ years | +45–55% |

Attachment method is the next major design choice. **Fully adhered systems** bond the membrane directly to the insulation with bonding adhesive — strongest wind resistance, ideal for high-wind environments like Dallas-Fort Worth and Denver, highest installed cost. **Mechanically attached systems** use fasteners and plates driven through the membrane to the structural deck — faster install, cost-effective for most general commercial applications. **Ballasted systems** use stone or pavers to hold the membrane in place — viable over concrete structures with adequate dead-load capacity, less common in our primary regions.

Insulation specification is inseparable from the membrane. Polyisocyanurate (polyiso) is the standard for most EPDM assemblies, paired with a high-density coverboard (polyiso, gypsum, or wood fiber) directly under the membrane. The coverboard improves puncture resistance from above and provides a stable substrate for adhesion. We pull cores during the pre-install assessment to verify existing insulation R-value and detect any moisture saturation — EPDM installed over wet insulation will fail early, and we don't take that gamble.

Flashing is where most EPDM roofs actually fail. Penetrations — HVAC curbs, vents, drains, parapet wall transitions — accumulate stress from thermal cycling, UV exposure, and incidental rooftop traffic. Pro Exteriors specifies Carlisle SynTec and Versico prefabricated flashing accessories wherever possible (factory-cured, dimensionally consistent, fewer field seams) and uses two-layer detail at every penetration: an uncured peel-and-stick base sheet topped by a field-bonded cap sheet, with photographic documentation of every detail.
