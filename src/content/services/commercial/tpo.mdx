## What TPO Roofing Actually Involves

Thermoplastic Polyolefin (TPO) is a single - ply membrane roof covering manufactured and delivered in rolls, applied to a commercial roof deck via fully adhered attachment, mechanical fastening or ballast attachment.

Different methods have different cost profiles, wind - uplift ratings and suitability for each specific roof deck assembly type.

Pro Exteriors chooses the method based on structural assessment of the building deck, local code requirements for wind uplift and seismic zones and suitability to accept added dead loads of such an assembly over time.

TPO membrane thickness is more significant for value than many realize.

Most new commercial installations default to 60 mil standard.

Heavy rooftop equipment and/or use areas, roofs prone to hailstorms (like Denver or Dallas - Fort Worth), high pedestrian areas and LEED - rated energy efficiency requirements may justify the upcharge for 80-mil or 90-mil thick membrane - which Pro Exteriors will specify if conditions warrant to protect the asset for the long term.

Properly installed seams with heat - welded bonds produce greater tensile strength than the membrane itself.

All of our seams are formed using fully calibrated automatic single - ply heat welders and specialized handheld welders at detailing areas where automatically - applied seams are impossible or insufficient.

The machinery we employ provides documentation for Carlisle, GAF and Versico audit acceptance.

We've never performed TPO roof installation work requiring or employing adhesive - based seam joining methods.

Like membrane thickness and attachment, Polyiso (polyisocyanurate) insulation panels are the primary choice when R - value per inch thickness is the priority, however, TPO jobs have a legitimate benefit from 4-way tapered insulation to help achieve or establish negative roof drainage, the use of special cover boards on roofs used as patios or that are subject to hail impacts and as hybrids utilizing Polyiso alongside EPS or other types of roof insulation for maximum effect as code requirements rise in your specific municipality.

Pro Exteriors has access to all Carlisle - manufactured roofing insulation and insulation assemblies, including every cover board option produced by Carlisle SynTec, which is used on virtually every TPO job when there's significant equipment placement, potential foot traffic or areas in zones that require increased impact and hail resistance.
