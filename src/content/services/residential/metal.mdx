## What Goes into a Residential Metal Roof Installation?

Installation of a metal residential roof is a multistage process that kicks off long before we ever pick up a panel to install. The exact details vary slightly depending on whether we're re - roofing an existing system over the deck, tearing off an existing asphalt system to prepare for a metal system or working on new construction.

The costs of materials, labor and timeline will be slightly different in each of these scenarios.

The first step in any metal roof project is decking evaluation. Metal requires a smooth, flat substrate upon which to install. This means that any weak areas in the decking, delaminated OSB or improperly spaced sheathing must be addressed and repaired before we ever touch a nail gun.

This is a crucial step for preventing issues later on and something we never skimp on to try to land a lower bid. If the deck needs repair, we clearly define that requirement, present an accurate quote for the work and get your explicit sign - off to move forward.

Unlike with asphalt roofs, underlayment selection is of much greater importance with metal roofs. We'll spec synthetic high - temperature underlayment products rated for the higher temperatures metal roofs reach under direct Texas sun.

Using standard felt isn't an option for these jobs and we do not use it for residential metal roofing. The underlayment works additionally as an extra moisture barrier in any locations where the roof is penetrated and where it meets the edges of the eaves. Depending on your home, your underlayment may vary from just one layer all the way up to three layers!

Choosing the right profile between standing seam metal panels or metal shingles influences both the visual aesthetics and the roofing's performance. With standing seam roofing, hidden fasteners ensure that the screws are contained entirely within the seam formed between adjacent panels, leading to far superior protection against wind and water damage.

This profile is also generally better suited to lower - slope residential roofs. Exposed - fastener metal shingles work nicely on steeper pitches, where they appear more traditional and have a better feel in older neighborhoods that retain their original architectural character.

We carry physical samples of both McElroy Metal and Atlas's metal products so that you can take them home to see how each will best integrate with your home's particular style before making the final selection. Flashing and trim are the primary source of metal roof failures in other businesses that attempt installation, in our business, it's one part where we never fail to excel.

Correct valley flashing, valley crickets, standard roof pipe flashing boot and drip edge can make a huge difference, requiring a high degree of precision craftsmanship and careful staging during installation. In cases where a standard trim piece can't be cut to the contours of your specific home or roof, our experienced crews will create custom trim on - site to guarantee a tight, weather - resistant fit.

No guesswork, no compromises, just good craftsmanship. At the conclusion of the project, your final inspection will be fully documented with a detailed set of photos of penetrations, all seams and your ridge work. The photo document package is an excellent resource for initiating an insurance claim if you ever need to do so and it gives you a record of exactly how your roof looked when we completed the installation.
