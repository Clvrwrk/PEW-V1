## What's included with our residential roof inspections?

Your average residential roof inspection includes more than just taking a peek at shingles from your lawn.

We examine all parts of the roof by first working our way around the home perimeter inspecting gutters and downspouts, fascia trim and soffits where there are obvious signs of trouble.

After all possible evidence of damage is located, it's photographed before a technician sets foot on your roof.

Once on the roof, inspection crews look for missing or displaced granules, failed nail seal - strips, pop - up nails, ridge caps missing granules or tabs and inspect the sealants surrounding pipe penetrations, skylights, chimneys step flashings and valley metal.

Penetrations are the highest rate failure spots and tend to get overlooked by inexperienced assessors.

Decking may also be exposed if there are missing tabs on the shingles and will be inspected for decking moisture as well as for its condition when a chunk of a tab is blown off of the roof.

If possible, technicians conduct interior attic checks, looking for evidence of daytime light leaking through the roof decking into your attic, water staining, displaced or improperly installed attic insulation and air conditioning leaks and we examine to see whether or not soffit - to - ridge balanced airflow exists, a known chronic problem for homes located in many Texas areas and Colorado locations.

Our inspectors produce a documented, itemized, zone - based photo report.

If hail or high wind evidence exists, we will include an estimated bill that's consistent with the information insurers expect to see.

If your roof is in satisfactory condition with no immediate need for extensive repairs, the inspectors will note this explicitly in the photo - documented report and provide you with an estimate for its remaining service life.
