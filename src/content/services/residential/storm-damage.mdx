## What Storm Damage Roofing Actually Involves

Storm damage roofing can be a wider category than most homeowners are aware of when they first reach out to Pro Exteriors.

Hail on asphalt shingles impacts under the top granule surface of the shingle and even though the damage isn't visible from the ground, it prematurely shortens the life span of the roof.

High wind on a shingle roof is experienced when tabs get lifted or break apart or the wind compromises sealing strips that provide resistance to blowing rain or moisture entering under the roof surface.

During extreme cold, ice damming on roofs causes meltwater to wick under the shingles, causing roof leaks even if it's not raining.

Even though ice damming in places such as Denver or Wichita will cause extensive damage inside the home, the roof may still look outwardly fine for some time.

Pro Exteriors always inspects the shingles, ridge cap shingles, all flashing valley surfaces and pipe boots.

Drip edge and gutters are checked and documented as are skylights and all other roof penetrations, like vent stacks.

We're committed to finding and documenting all hail- or wind - damaged areas.

If we find areas where moisture has soaked into your roof decking or soft spots on your plywood or OSB underlayment, we document that as well.

Why? Shingles over a soft deck will eventually fail again on a short schedule.

When it comes to selecting materials, GAF and CertainTeed provide us with shingles on almost all roofs we handle.

While these companies don't offer as extensive choices, both have impact - resistant Class 4 shingles.

If you live in Texas, Colorado, Kansas or Missouri, installing one of these impact - rated shingles can lead to insurance discounts on your policy.

Atlas also carries great - impact - resistant options, so we can help meet any project needs you've.

Your choice of underlayment, ice - and - water shield and starter strips are climate - appropriate selections selected based on the specific weather - prone region where your property is located.
