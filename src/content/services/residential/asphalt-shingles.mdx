### What Asphalt Shingle Roof Replacement Actually Entails

While replacing an asphalt shingle roof might seem to be merely swapping shingles layer for layer, a correctly installed shingle system replacement is actually a system install with a series of predetermined installation sequences.

Work begins with a complete rip of all previous materials.

Layering shingles over old ones isn't just dangerous due to potentially trapped moisture and warranty issues but also risky due to the possibility of missed, unseen decking problems that could prove costly to rectify in the future.

Once the roof deck is completely visible, our crews investigate every piece of sheathing for signs of decay, delamination or misplaced screws.

Damaged decking will be replaced prior to the initiation of any subsequent layers.

For a Dallas - Fort Worth or Atlanta summer, with temperatures exceeding 150°F on most attic floors, the majority of our jobs will now have synthetic underlayment material that boasts a higher rating for thermal expansion.

However, for ice damming issues commonly seen in a Denver or Wichita spring or fall season, an additional underlayment product called the ice - and - water shield will be installed at all eave lines, along valleys and above roof penetrations before we overlay the rest of the roof field with a layer of synthetic material.

Starter strips and drip edge are properly placed prior to laying the field shingles on the roof, followed by valley treatments that range from closed - cut or open metal installations, to weave Valley - specific installations depending on roof pitch and rainfall intensity.

Hip and ridge caps will be put in place to complement the same shingles used in the field so that your warranty coverage is uninterrupted.

New pipe boots and sealants will also be installed, while skylights and step flashings will either be replaced with new parts or properly resealed over existing.

Each job, we sweep the ground with magnetic roller units to locate stray screws, then submit a complete photo package to you, the homeowner.

If you're currently navigating an insurance claim related to residential storm damage, our project managers can work with insurance adjusters directly to clarify details on scope accuracy and coverage allowances.
