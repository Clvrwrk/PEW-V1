# What Highland Park Residential Roofing Entails

A residential roofing job in Highland Park involves more than a standard shingle replacement. We start with a complete review of the existing deck, flashing (both the fascia and soffit) and ventilation within the attic. Installing shingles over a deck that is damaged or over an attic that's not sufficiently ventilated negates all manufacturer warranties and requires a fix in three years.

After the inspection, we give a documented scope, listing every task: the removal of the shingles and other old materials, repairing the roof deck (if required), putting ice - and - water barrier protection along the edges of the eaves and any valleys, installing synthetic underlayment over the complete roofing area, drip edges, ridge caps and the necessary flashing at any openings like chimneys, vents and sky lights. You'll not find vaguely described charges on your scope. Instead, you get a complete breakdown of what you pay for before we remove any of the existing shingles.

We also fabricate custom step flashing and counter flashing for homes with chimneys (common in older Highland Park styles, such as Tudors and Colonial Revivals). Unlike relying on pieces formed off the production line, we can bend flashing as needed right at the job site. The most frequent issue reported by homeowners in Highland Park with their roof is a leaky chimney, which proper flashing remedies.

We tackle ventilation for each roof project. The calculation of your net free ventilation area and the amount of square feet inside your attic allows us to correctly position and size Lomanco or Air Vent ridge vents specifically for the contours of your roof. Proper ventilation can add between 15-20% to your shingle's longevity and manage the internal heat load during summer, which becomes important for Highland Park homes over 4,000 square feet in size.

For residential projects, we select materials from industry partners like GAF, CertainTeed and Atlas for shingles. We use Soprema or Carlisle products if you've any areas requiring low - slope roof membranes, such as additions with flat roofs. All our materials are ordered directly from the manufacturer, we don't source them from wholesale suppliers.
