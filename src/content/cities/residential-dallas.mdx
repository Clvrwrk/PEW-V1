### What Does Residential Roofing in Dallas Actually Entail?

Residential roofing in Dallas is a comprehensive service that generally goes far beyond merely replacing shingles. The full scope of the work typically commences with a thorough roof inspection. This inspection might be a pre - sale assessment, a post - storm damage evaluation or a routine check on a roof nearing the end of its intended lifespan. We employ drone imaging for the majority of our inspections, capturing detailed views of every roof plane, valley and roof penetrations before ever stepping foot onto the roof itself.

For replacement projects, the subsequent step is the tear - off. We completely strip the existing roofing material down to the decking. Every sheet of OSB or plywood is then carefully examined for any signs of softening, delamination or prior water damage. Any compromised sections are replaced before new underlayment is installed. We use synthetic underlayment as our standard material, as it offers superior resistance to heat and moisture compared to traditional felt underlayment. This is crucial given that a Dallas summer can cause attic temperatures to exceed 160°F.

Flashing is a common point where lower - quality installations often fail. We meticulously replace the step flashing at every wall junction, re - flash all pipe boots using either lead or high - temperature rubber collars depending on the diameter of the pipe and install pre - formed metal valley flashings than woven shingles in open valleys. The ridge ventilation system is sized according to the square footage of the attic, than being approximated, ensuring proper exhaust ventilation which extends the life of the shingles and helps maintain manageable cooling loads.

Our trusted partners for residential roofing materials include GAF, CertainTeed and Atlas for shingles. For specialty applications, such as flat surfaces above garages or covered patios, we collaborate with Duro - Last or Versico TPO membranes, opting against torch - down modified bitumen due to its fire risks in residential environments. Every project concludes with a thorough cleaning of the property, a pass with a magnetic roller to collect any stray fasteners and a documented final inspection with photographic evidence delivered to the homeowner.
