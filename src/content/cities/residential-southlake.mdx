 ## What Residential Roofing Southlake Actually Involves

 A residential roofing project in Southlake typically involves an exhaustive inspection, going beyond a quick walkthrough. Our thorough assessment includes the decking, all flashing points (chimneys, valleys, walls), ridges and attic ventilation. We photograph every issue identified and include it in our written scope of work before starting any shingle removal.

 For full roof replacements, we completely remove old shingles down to the deck. Our team inspects the decking for soft spots and signs of water damage, replacing any compromised decking before proceeding with new materials. We use synthetic underlayment as standard practice than felt. Furthermore, ice - and - water shield is applied to all valleys, eaves and around penetrations as a default, regardless of the minimum city code requirement. The minimum code is a floor for safety, not the specification for quality.

 Common material choices for Southlake homeowners include GAF Timberline HDZ or CertainTeed Landmark Pro architectural shingles. Both offer Class 4 impact resistance when installed correctly with suitable underlayments - a significant advantage for those aiming to reduce their insurance premiums. For steeper roof pitches or to complement standing seam metal details, we can obtain materials from McElroy Metal and can seamlessly blend asphalt roofing shingles with metal panels.

 Flashing is the root of most roof leaks and deserves our concentrated effort. Step flashing against vertical walls, counter flashing around chimneys and kick - out flashing at wall - to - roof interfaces are all fabricated from sheet metal, never sealed with caulk. Caulk requires frequent maintenance, metal flashing is part of a durable system.

 Cleanup is an integral part of the job, not an afterthought. We use a magnetic sweep across the yard to collect all fallen nails after tear - off, dispose of all debris on the same day and leave the site as clean as we found it - or cleaner.
