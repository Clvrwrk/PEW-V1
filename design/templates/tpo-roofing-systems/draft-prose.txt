### service_overview.body ###
TPO is the fastest-growing single-ply commercial roofing system in North
America for a reason. It pairs the durability of EPDM with the chemical
resistance of PVC — without the price premium of either. For a commercial
property in the Dallas-Fort Worth metro, where a roof has to survive 100°F
summers and golf-ball hail in the same calendar year, that combination is
the practical answer.

Pro Exteriors installs TPO with hot-air welded seams, not adhesive tape.
The seam becomes the strongest part of the roof rather than the weakest —
a measurable difference in wind-uplift and leak resistance over the life
of the membrane. (Want the full materials breakdown? See our
<a href="/blog/tpo-vs-epdm-vs-pvc">TPO vs EPDM vs PVC comparison</a>.)

### benefit.reflective-efficiency ###
White TPO membranes reflect roughly 70–80% of incoming solar radiation,
cutting cooling load on the building below. On a Dallas commercial
property the cooling-cost reduction lands somewhere between 15% and
30%, depending on insulation and HVAC age. [REPRESENTATIVE — NOT YET
SOURCED for Pro Exteriors specifically; industry range from
Cool Roof Rating Council and ENERGY STAR.]

### benefit.puncture-resistant ###
A reinforcing scrim laminated between the polyolefin layers gives TPO
meaningful resistance to debris strikes, foot traffic from HVAC
servicers, and the occasional wind-blown branch. Hailstones pit the
surface; they don't perforate it.

### benefit.cost-effective ###
Material costs run $0.70–$1.30 per square foot for the membrane alone,
with installed costs typically $4–$10 per square foot depending on
attachment method, insulation, and access. Compared to PVC, TPO
delivers comparable durability at 20–30% lower installed cost.

### benefit.lifespan ###
Properly specified and maintained, a quality TPO system performs for
20–30 years before warranty replacement comes into play. Manufacturer
warranties run 15–25 years; system performance often outlasts the
paper.

### benefit.eco-friendly ###
TPO is a thermoplastic — it can be re-melted and reformed at the end
of its service life rather than landfilled. Several membrane
manufacturers operate take-back programs for tear-off material.

### benefit.leak-proof-seams ###
Hot-air welded TPO seams are up to four times stronger than taped
EPDM seams under peel-and-shear testing. The seam stops being the
weakest point of the roof and becomes the strongest. (See our
<a href="/blog/tpo-vs-epdm-vs-pvc">complete TPO vs EPDM breakdown</a>
for the full seam-test data.)

### application.industrial ###
Distribution warehouses, manufacturing plants, and logistics centers
with 50,000+ square feet of low-slope roof area. TPO installs fast
across long runs, the white surface helps keep dock-door temperatures
manageable, and the seam-strength matters when forklift drivers are
running roof-mounted equipment.

### application.retail ###
Strip malls, big-box anchors, and mixed-use developments where the
roof has to be installed in phases without disrupting tenant
operations. Hot-air welding produces no fumes — overlay work can
proceed during business hours when other systems can't. (Multi-family?
See <a href="/residential-roofing">residential roof options</a>.)

### application.residential-complex ###
Multi-family apartments, modern flat-roof homes, and mixed-use podium
residential. TPO's reflective surface meaningfully reduces summer
cooling complaints from top-floor units — a real ROI signal in a
market where tenant retention drives the underwriting.

### quality.step1 ###
Detailed structural analysis of the existing deck, moisture scanning
to map any wet insulation or compromised substrate, and uplift-rating
verification against the local wind-zone code. The assessment gets
documented and stays in the property's maintenance file. We do not

### quality.step2 ###
Custom-cut membrane and flashing details to ensure zero-gap
integration with HVAC curbs, vents, drains, and parapet walls. Every
penetration on the roof is pre-templated and field-verified.
Reinforcing scrim is fully encapsulated at every transition.

### quality.step3 ###
20-point final inspection covering seam-weld integrity (probe-tested),
flashing termination, drainage, and warranty-eligible adherence to
manufacturer specs. Inspection report is delivered with the warranty
package — and stays with the property if it sells.

### faq: How long does a TPO roof last? ###
With proper installation and routine maintenance, a quality TPO system
delivers 20–30 years of service life. Manufacturer warranties typically
run 15–25 years; in our experience, system performance often outlasts
the paper warranty when twice-yearly inspections are followed.

### faq: Can TPO be installed over an existing ro ###
Often yes — TPO overlay (or "recover") installs are common when the
existing substrate is dry, structurally sound, and within local code
for total roof system weight. Pro Exteriors' pre-install assessment
includes moisture scanning and structural analysis specifically to
determine whether overlay is the right call versus a full tear-off.
Overlay installs are typically 30–40% cheaper than full replacement
when conditions allow.

### faq: Is TPO the same as PVC? ###
No. Both are thermoplastic single-ply membranes with similar surface
appearance, but the chemistry, cost, and performance differ. PVC
outperforms TPO in chemical-exposure environments (restaurants,
chemical plants) and at extreme temperatures, but typically costs
20–30% more installed. TPO is the better choice for most general
commercial applications. For the full breakdown see our
<a href="/blog/tpo-vs-epdm-vs-pvc">TPO vs EPDM vs PVC comparison</a>.

### faq: What color options are available? ###
White is the standard and recommended choice — it delivers the
reflective efficiency that drives most of TPO's energy-saving value.
Gray and tan are available from major manufacturers when aesthetics
require a non-white roof (e.g., visible roof from a higher building).
Custom colors are possible with extended manufacturer lead time but
rarely worth the cost premium for a low-slope commercial roof that
isn't visually prominent.
