### FAQ ###
How long does a TPO roof last?
When installed correctly and properly maintained on a bi - annual basis, you should expect your TPO system to provide 20 to 30 years of service life. Most manufacturers' warranties run for 15 to 25 years. While the warranty is a baseline value, we've seen most properly maintained TPO roofs outlast their written warranties.

Can TPO be installed over an existing roof?
In many cases, yes. When the existing roofing substrate is dry, structurally sound and doesn't violate any local codes about the weight limit of roof system totals, a TPO "cover" installation (or overlay) is typically feasible. The comprehensive moisture scanning and structural analysis included in each pre - installation assessment by Pro Exteriors is key to determining whether an overlay is suitable versus a complete roof system tear - off. An overlay, when conditions allow, usually offers savings of approximately 30-40% over a full replacement.

Is TPO the same as PVC?
No. While both are single - ply thermoplastics that may share similar aesthetics from a distance, the chemical makeup, price point and level of performance they offer differ . PVC typically offers higher durability when exposed to chemicals (e. g. , restaurants, chemical plants) and can perform better in more extreme temperatures. However, PVC usually costs about 20-30% more to install. TPO is a much more practical solution for most of the commercial buildings across the DFW metroplex. For a more detailed comparison, see <a href="/blog/tpo - vs - epdm - vs - pvc">TPO vs EPDM vs PVC: Which Commercial Membrane Is Right for You? </a>

What color options are available?
White is the standard and most recommended option. It's a crucial component in providing the reflective efficiency that makes TPO such an excellent energy - saving solution. However, many of the top manufacturers offer gray and tan membranes for situations where the building's aesthetics demand a non - white roof (e. g. , a visible roof line from a taller adjacent office building or condo). It's possible to special order custom colors from some manufacturers, but this usually comes with significant delays and can often exceed the cost - benefit ratio for a low - slope commercial roof not in plain view.
