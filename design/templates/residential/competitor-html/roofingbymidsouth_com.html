Frequently Asked Questions

  • 1. What license is needed for residential roofing in Nashville?

    Any roofing job more than $25,000 requires a General Contractor License in the State of TN. In order to obtain a GC license, a trade exam, as well as a business and law exam must be passed. Proof of financial stability and references must also be provided and approved. For jobs valued below $25,000, a Handyman or BC-21 Roofing License is sufficient. The Handyman and BC-21 License do not have a trade exam and the financial requirements are substantially lower.

  • 2. Do residential roofing contractors guarantee their work?

    If you hire a reputable residential roofing company, they should guarantee their workmanship for a specified period of time. This should cover the contractor’s labor and material that is required to fix any issues that are caused by their workmanship.

  • 3. What does a roof warranty cover?

    Roof Warranties for residential roofing in Nashville will vary between roofing contractors and manufacturers. The most common type of roof warranty is the manufacturer warranty that only covers defective materials. Reputable roofing contractors will also provide a workmanship warranty which typically runs for 2-5 years. GAF Master Elite® Certified roofing contractors can also provide Silver Pledge and Golden Pledge warranties that provide a 10 year of workmanship warranty guaranteed by the manufacturer.

  • 4. My roof is leaking. Do I need the whole thing replaced?

    Usually no. A roof leak can typically be repaired with an affordable roof repair. However, you should always consult a reputable residential roofing company to ensure that your roof leak is not the result of storm damage. If so, your roof damage may be covered by your insurance company and a full roof replacement could be warranted.

  • 7. When should I pay my roofer?

    If your roof replacement, handled by our Nashville residential roofing company, is covered by your insurance, you should expect to pay your deductible before your roof is replaced. The balance will be collected by your roofer upon completion. For retail agreements, a deposit will be based on the size of the project, time frame, and type of material. Custom ordered materials require a larger deposit. In general, you should expect to pay up to 50% down for retail agreements for roof replacements. For more details, read our blog post on When Should My Roofer Ask for Money?

  • 8. How can I determine the work my roof needs, such as repairs vs replacement?

As a leading residential roofing company with expert residential roofing contractors and extensive experience, we have the skills to carefully inspect your roof and provide an honest recommendation. Book a Nashville residential roofing inspection to determine exactly what your roof needs.

  • 9. Will you need to enter the house when working on my roof?
