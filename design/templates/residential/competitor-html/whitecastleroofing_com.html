How long will it take to replace my roof?

It depends on the size and complexity of the roof. An average-sized roof is typically 15,000 to 25,000 SF and can usually be completed in one day. It may take longer if you have a very complex or large roof. Extra time may be needed on average-sized roofs if multiple shingle layers need to be removed.

What time of year is the best for a roof replacement?

It is best to replace a roof when the temperatures are between 40°F and 90°F. If the temperature is outside this range, extra time and care must be taken.

What should I do to prepare for my roofing project?

Keeping your yard free from pets, children, and personal items would be best until the roofing project is complete. If you have a fenced-in yard, please keep your gates unlocked for our team until the project is complete. Get all the details about roofing preparation day: what you should do and what you can expect.

What is the difference between an asphalt shingle and an architectural shingle?

Asphalt shingles are any shingle that consists of either a fiberglass or felt paper base coated with a waterproof layer of asphalt and topped with ceramic granules.

Architectural shingles are simply a specific type of asphalt shingle. They are unique in their 2-ply design, which gives them an exquisite, three-dimensional appearance.

That’s why homeowners most commonly choose architectural shingles to enhance their homes’ visual appeal.

The other standard style of asphalt shingle is known as a “3-tab shingle.” 3-tab shingles are aptly named since they are designed as a single-ply shingle with three tabs. They provide a classic shingle look but are less aesthetically pleasing than architectural shingles.
