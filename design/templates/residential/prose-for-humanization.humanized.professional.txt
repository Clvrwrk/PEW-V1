Three key factors are most influential in determining whether a roof is best suited for repair versus full replacement: the age of the roof, the pattern of the damage and the degree of shingle granule loss. A roof less than 10 years old showing isolated damage is usually a good candidate for repair. Conversely, a roof exceeding 20 years with widespread granule loss across the entire surface, multiple leak sites or hail impacts distributed over more than 30% of the field is generally a clear indicator that a replacement is necessary.
